Who we are, and what we do

Welcome to SpeedSter-Motors, we endeavour to provide the best service and be market leaders when it comes to everything relating to cars. The journey to autonomous cars may still seem impossibly futuristic. Their coming debut will take our hands off the wheel and has followed an evolutionary path that stretches back to Leonardo da Vinci and beyond. Companies such as Ford, Mercedes and Tesla are racing to build autonomous vehicles for a radically changing consumer world. Ford, for instance, recently tripled its investment in its autonomous vehicle fleet and is testing 30 autonomous Ford Fusion hybrids in California, Michigan and Arizona. And yet, the fingerprints of tech history can be seen in almost every aspect of their exciting new capabilities.

History of the Autonomous car

  • da Vinci’s Self-Propelled Cart—c. 1500
  • Whitehead Torpedo—1868 While these weapons of war emerged in the mid-1700s
  • POYEJ - Mechanical Mike aircraft autopilot—1933 Extended travel times and led to autopilots
  • Teetor Cruise Control—1945, developed the first cruise control
  • Stanford Cart—1961 With the space race in full swing, researchers, vehicles going to the moon
  • STANFORD UNIVERSITY - Tsukuba Mechanical Engineering—1977, vehicle-mounted cameras
  • VaMoRs—1987 major milestone in autonomous tech came from German engineer Ernst Dickmanns, who equipped a sedan with a bank of cameras and 60 micro-processing modules to detect objects on the road—in front of and behind the vehicle
  • General Atomics MQ-1 Predator—1995 While we tend to think of autonomous vehicles as a means of converting humans from drivers to passengers
  • U.S. AIR FORCE PHOTO/LT COL LESLIE PRATT, DARPA Challenges—2004-2013 The U.S. Department of Defense’s research arm, DARPA, challenge sponsorships
  • Tesla Autopilot—2015 The most significant aspect of Tesla’s semi-autonomous “Autopilot” feature, introduced in late 2015—which enabled hands-free control for highway and freeway driving— is that it was delivered in the form of a single software update to Model S owners overnight
  • University of Michigan’s MCity—2015 The University of Michigan’s 32-acre Mobility Transformation Center, called MCity, launched in 2015 as a world-class test facility for autonomous vehicle technology. Ford became the first automaker to test autonomous vehicles there, and in the harshest environmental conditions imaginable
  • The most exciting part of the dawn of the autonomous vehicle era is the collaboration between private industry, government, and academia using AI, as quoted by Andrew NG
